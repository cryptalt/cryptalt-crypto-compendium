def toggle_code(state):

    """
    Toggles the JavaScript show()/hide() function on the div.input element.
    """

    output_string = "<script>$(\"div.input\").{}</script>"
    output_args   = (javascript_functions[state],)

    value.owner.description = button_descriptions[state]

Cognitive Biases¶

Incentive Bias — Conflict of interest¶

Be extremely skeptical of any recommendation to buy anything. Many or most of those shilling projects have powerful incentives to lie to you, and themselves about the future of any project. Most projects will not "go to the moon", and will eventually go to $0.

“If you can't understand why someone is doing something, look at the consequences of their actions, whatever they might be, and then infer the motivations from their consequences.”
—Jordan Peterson

Media and social media are filled with paid promotions, many undisclosed. They are either being paid to lie to you, or being paid to dump their payment in coins on you. If someone is telling you to buy, they probably bought it at a much lower price.

"Consider the presentations of brokers selling commercial real estate and businesses. I’ve never seen one that I thought was even within hailing distance of objective truth. In my long life, I have never seen a management consultant’s report that didn’t end with the same advice: “This problem needs more management consulting services.” Widespread incentive-caused bias requires that one should often distrust, or take with a grain of salt, the advice of one’s professional advisor, even if he is an engineer.
The general antidotes here are:
(1) especially fear professional advice when it is especially good for the advisor;
(2) learn and use the basic elements of your advisor’s trade as you deal with your advisor; and
(3) double check, disbelieve, or replace much of what you’re told, to the degree that seems appropriate after objective thought."

—Charlie Munger, The Psychology of Human Misjudgement

Compound interest is the most powerful force in the universe. He who understands it, earns it; he who doesn’t, pays it.

Automated strategies for Buying¶

Lump Sum Investing¶

The best time to buy Bitcoin was 5 years ago. The second best time is now.

With lump sum investing, you invest a large sum and never sell, with the goal of maximizing time in the market. Lump sum also involves timing the market. Effective lump sum investing requires timing the market. Since crypto prices regularly crash 30% or more, you don't want to go all-in when prices are near an all-time high.

Dollar Cost Averaging (DCA)¶

With dollar cost averaging, you purchase at a regular interval, e.g. weekly, monthly. This can be an extremely effective strategy, especially for new investors or those with limited time or capital. If you are new to investing, or have been losing money trading, strongly consider this strategy.

Advantages of dollar cost averaging

  • don't need to time the market
  • very little time required
  • limits risk
  • trains to "buy the dip" instead of panic sell
  • uses volatility to lower cost basis

Dollar cost averaging may even outperform a lump sum if the lump sum was bought near a new all time high, as in the December 2017 example below.

Market cycle indicators¶

Logarithmic Regression - BTC/ETH rainbow chart¶

In this strategy, you buy when the price of Bitcoin or Ethereum is significantly below it's logarithmic regression trend (blue/green) and you sell when it's significantly above (orange/red). This strategy can be combined with either lump sum or weighted dollar cost averaging, where the amount you buy increases as the price is "cheap" on the regression model.

There is no guarantee this regression model will hold in the future.

Source: https://www.blockchaincenter.net/bitcoin-rainbow-chart/

See also: https://www.blockchaincenter.net/ethereum-rainbow-chart/

Pi Cycle Top Indicator¶

In this strategy, you wait for the value of the 350 daily moving average x2 (orange line) to pass the 111 daily moving averagee (green line) as a sell signal. This indicator has often timed market tops to within a few days.
